Summary of typography changes Hero headline — “Notes from places that ask you to slow down.” Font stack now leads with "Baskerville" (native on macOS / iOS) and falls back to "Libre Baskerville" (loaded from Google Fonts) and then to Georgia. Kept upright (no italic), font-weight: 400, calm sizing. Slightly increased line-height to 1.3 because Baskerville reads more elegantly with a touch more breathing room than Georgia. Filter labels — COUNTRY / EXPERIENCE Switched to the same Baskerville / Libre Baskerville font stack. Letter-spacing widened to 0.42em to match the airy, gallery-card feeling of references like "GEORGIA & CHARLIE". Slightly smaller font-size (0.72rem) and a calmer warm-brown color (#4a3f36) — refined editorial navigation rather than a UI label. Filter chips — country & experience pills Now use Baskerville at a comfortable 0.95rem reading size, normal letter-spacing, weight 400. Borders softened to a low-opacity warm hairline (rgba(74, 63, 54, 0.28)) and border-radius reduced to 1px for a flatter, more printed-publication feel — no longer reads as an app pill. Active and hover states still resolve to the same warm-black fill so the editorial mood holds even when interacting. Preserved exactly as before Logo Faraway Postcards (Georgia, wide-tracked, ivory on warm black) — completely untouched. Body paragraph beginning "Faraway Postcards is an independent travel journal…" — completely untouched. Layout, colors, hero image, spacing, sticky header, article feed, footer. A note on rendering: Apple devices ship a native Baskerville and will render the text in that elegant transitional serif immediately. On Windows and Android, where Baskerville isn't installed, the page falls through to Libre Baskerville loaded from Google Fonts — a faithful and well-respected open-source revival that holds the same character. This guarantees a consistent editorial typography across every device without depending on a single platform.
